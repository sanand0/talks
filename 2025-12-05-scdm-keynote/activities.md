# Activities

1. **Validate informed consent forms with ChatGPT against the ICH E6 guideline**
  - Who: speaker
  - When: during the talk
  - Model/tool: ChatGPT
  - What happened: Anand uploaded the guideline and asked ChatGPT to list non-compliances in five informed consent forms.
  - Source:
    > `Let's upload the ICH E6 guideline into ChatGPT and ask it to validate the following five informed consent forms`
    >
    > `After 2 min 41 seconds of thinking, it found 15 errors.`

2. **Generate a checklist from a protocol document with Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT**
  - Who: speaker
  - When: during the talk
  - Model/tool: Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT
  - What happened: Anand used an LLM to ingest a document and turn it into a checklist / SOP for review.
  - Source:
    > `Let's say we want to create a checklist. What we could do is just send Claude or Gemini or ChatGPT the file and say, "Now give me a checklist."`
    >
    > `Effectively creating an SOP.`

3. **Double-check model outputs by passing one model's result to another**
  - Who: speaker
  - When: during the talk
  - Model/tool: multiple models
  - What happened: Anand described using multiple models to cross-check classifications and reduce error rates.
  - Source:
    > `what if we took the results of one model, passed it to another model, and asked it to cross-check?`
    >
    > `the default error rate of something like around 14% falls to about 3.5% with double checking.`

  - Source:

  - Who: speaker
