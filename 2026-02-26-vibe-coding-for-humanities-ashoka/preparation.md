Ashoka University offers a broad, multidisciplinary liberal arts and sciences education. The humanities disciplines are housed under the **Harish and Bina Shah School of Humanities**, which encompasses departments such as English, History, Philosophy, Performing Arts, Creative Writing, and Visual Arts.
The undergraduate curriculum (B.A. Honours / Honours with Research) spans four years in alignment with the National Education Policy and requires students to complete at least 160 credits to graduate. The curriculum is divided into four main pillars:

1. Foundation Courses (36 Credits)
   Before diving into their specializations, all students must complete 9 mandatory foundation courses. These are designed to build core intellectual skills and expose students to diverse styles of thinking. The foundation courses include:
   - Introduction to Critical Thinking
   - Great Books
   - Literature and the World
   - Indian Civilizations
   - Environmental Studies
   - Mind and Behaviour
   - Economy, Politics, and Society
   - Mathematical Thinking
   - Principles of Science
2. Major Courses (Minimum 72 Credits)
   Students typically declare their major by their third semester. A pure major usually requires 12 courses (48 credits), while interdisciplinary majors can require up to 16 courses.
   - **Pure Humanities Majors:** Include programs like English, History, and Philosophy. These consist of "gateway" introductory courses, core survey courses, and specialized advanced electives.
   - **Interdisciplinary Majors:** Include combinations like History and International Relations, English and Creative Writing, English and Media Studies, and Politics, Philosophy, and Economics (PPE).
3. Co-Curricular Courses & Internships
   To ensure a well-rounded education, students must complete 2-credit co-curricular courses that blend practical training with critical analysis. Offerings typically include performing arts (theatre, music, dance), visual arts, or languages (such as French, Latin, or Urdu). Students are also required to complete a minimum of 2 credits worth of internships to graduate.
4. Minors and Concentrations (Optional)
   Ashoka allows students great flexibility to explore interests outside of their primary major. Students can opt to pursue a **Minor**, which requires 6 courses (24 credits) in an outside subject, or a **Concentration**, which requires 4 courses. This allows students to mix distinct fields, such as majoring in History while minoring in Visual Arts or Creative Writing.
