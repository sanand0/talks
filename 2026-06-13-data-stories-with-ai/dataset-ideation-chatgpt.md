## Best practical picks

**1. Oil → wheel → wallet.**
This is the best all-rounder. It satisfies oil imports, war impact, stock markets, car sales, fuel type, consumer spending, sector performance and business analysis. The non-obvious insight: vehicle registrations are a better “middle-class stress sensor” than GDP. Two-wheeler sales, SUV sales, EV adoption, commercial vehicle registrations and oil-import shifts tell different parts of the same story.

**2. Years of life lost, degrees gained.**
This combines Delhi summer, AQI, WHO-style pollution data, life expectancy and state-level demographic change. The non-obvious insight: don’t show AQI; show “how many life-years are at stake” and “which states age before they get rich.”

**3. Sports as uncertainty theatre.**
This is the safest live workshop dataset. IPL and FIFA are instantly understandable, emotionally engaging, and ideal for showing how AI can write stories from structured data. The non-obvious insight: the real lesson is not “who will win,” but how probabilities, uncertainty and narratives diverge.

**4. The early-warning machine.**
Use LMS data, grievances, supplier renewals and predictive maintenance as one reusable template. The non-obvious insight: “student will drop out,” “supplier will fail,” “grievance will breach SLA,” and “engine will fail” are all survival/risk-ranking problems.

**5. Election performance without lazy caste inference.**
This is powerful but needs care. Use ECI, Lok Dhaba and ADR/MyNeta, but avoid pretending that caste can be inferred reliably from names. The non-obvious insight: party performance is not just vote share; it is candidate selection, incumbency, reserved seats, wealth/criminality profiles, media attention and repeatability.

## Best wildcards

**Wildcard 1: Himalayan gender-forest negotiation map.**
High novelty, slower execution. Build it from Forest Survey of India district forest cover, NFHS district indicators, Census-style rural/tribal variables and qualitative context. The non-obvious insight: forests are not just ecology; they are unpaid labor, mobility, fuel, water, livelihood and bargaining power.

**Wildcard 2: World cuisine as an ingredient migration graph.**
Fast and fun. Use RecipeNLG/Food.com/TheMealDB to map cuisines as ingredient networks. The non-obvious insight: cuisines are not categories; they are “business models” for transforming local constraints into repeatable delight.

**Wildcard 3: Actors turned politicians — fame decay curve.**
Use Wikidata to identify actors, then ECI/Lok Dhaba/PRS-style political performance proxies. The non-obvious insight: celebrity may help entry, but administration requires a different capability stack. The failure risk is defining “good administrator” in a defensible way.

## What I would put in front of the audience

Give them five choices:

1. “Can oil shocks predict what Indians buy?”
2. “When will India start shrinking — and which states shrink first?”
3. “How many years of life does Delhi’s air and heat cost?”
4. “Can IPL and FIFA teach us probability better than textbooks?”
5. “Can AI detect failure before it happens — in students, suppliers, machines and governments?”

The best single workshop pick is **Sports as uncertainty theatre** if you need instant engagement. The best executive/business pick is **Oil → wheel → wallet**. The best serious public-policy pick is **Years of life lost, degrees gained**.
