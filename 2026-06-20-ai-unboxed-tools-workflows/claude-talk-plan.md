### Workflows

Since you asked for input on structure: one spine that works for this audience is **Pattern → Apply → Harden** — first teach a handful of reusable workflow _shapes_, then show each applied to a finance/strategy task they recognize, then close on turning the keepers into skills/connectors (your compounding-asset close). Below is the broad menu to pick from; everything is one line.

**The reusable shapes (the conceptual core)**

- Chaining — break a task into ordered steps where each output feeds the next.
- Router — classify an input first, then send it down the right path.
- Fan-out / fan-in — generate several drafts or angles in parallel, then synthesize the best.
- Critique-and-revise — have the model review and improve its own first pass before you see it.
- Generate-then-verify — produce fast, then check against a source or rule (your Verify Button / Verifiable Autonomy).
- Extract → structure → analyze — turn messy documents into tables, then reason over them.
- Research → synthesize → produce — gather sources, distill, and emit a finished artifact in one flow.
- Expert lens / persona — frame the task through a specific expert's priorities before answering (your skill).
- Memory accumulation — let context build across sessions so the model gets sharper on a recurring topic.
- Scheduled / recurring — run a brief or scan automatically on a cadence rather than on demand.
- Multi-agent orchestration — a lead delegating subtasks to specialist agents, which the new managed-agent runtimes now support.
- Skill-ification — capture a prompt you keep reusing as a versioned skill so it fires consistently.

**Personal productivity**

- Inbox triage that labels, summarizes, and drafts replies for your sign-off.
- A morning brief that merges calendar, unread priorities, and overnight news into one note.
- Meeting prep that builds a one-page persona card on whoever you're about to meet (your practice).
- Post-meeting capture that turns a transcript into decisions, owners, and follow-up drafts.
- A weekly review that rolls up what moved, what slipped, and what's next.
- A reading pipeline that converts long PDFs into a summary plus an audio overview for the commute.
- Travel and expense handling from forwarded confirmations and photographed receipts.

**Knowledge & research**

- Competitive and market intelligence assembled from public sources on a schedule.
- Regulatory and policy scanning relevant to finance and fintech in the region.
- Source-grounded Q&A over a fixed document set to keep answers honest (NotebookLM).
- Deep-research reports with citations for a topic you're going deep on.
- Earnings-call and filing analysis distilled into a one-page thesis.
- Due-diligence dossiers compiled from data rooms, filings, and news.
- A recurring "AI field notes" digest you publish from your own reading (your newsletter).

**Communication & writing**

- Drafting in your own voice from a captured style guide (your writing-style skill).
- Email variants by strategy — e.g., "hold firm" vs. "offer an alternative" — to pick from.
- Board and exec memos generated from raw notes against a house template.
- Turning a deck into a tight spoken narrative, or a narrative into a deck.
- Thought-leadership and LinkedIn drafts spun from a transcript of you talking.
- Localization and translation across the languages this multilingual market needs.
- Reframing a hard message (a decline, a pushback) with the right tone.

**Data & analysis**

- Conversational analysis of a CSV or Excel file with charts generated on the fly.
- Natural-language querying of a warehouse via the BigQuery connector.
- Dashboards and one-off visualizations built straight from raw exports.
- Data storytelling that turns a finding into a narrative a non-technical board will remember (your data-story skill).
- Lightweight financial modeling and scenario tables from a prompt.
- Survey, NPS, and open-feedback synthesis into themes and quotes.
- Anomaly and pattern hunting across a dataset to surface what's surprising.

**Creation & media**

- Prompt-to-deck for a first draft you then refine (Gamma/Canva/NotebookLM).
- Explainer videos from a document for onboarding or stakeholder updates (Google Vids/NotebookLM).
- Podcast-style audio overviews of dense material for passive consumption.
- Infographics and one-pagers generated from a report.
- Diagrams and flowcharts from a text description (Mermaid).
- On-brand images for posts and decks without a designer.
- A throwaway interactive app or calculator built as an artifact to solve a one-off need.

**Building & automation (light/no-code)**

- A custom GPT, Gem, or Project that packages a recurring assistant for your team.
- An AI-powered artifact that calls the model itself for a small internal tool (your "Claude-in-Claude" demo).
- A scheduled background agent that runs a scan or report unattended.
- A Zapier/Make flow where an AI step sits between two apps to triage or summarize.
- A form-to-pipeline setup where submissions get auto-processed and routed.
- An internal MCP that exposes a private corpus or system to the model.

**Role-specific (tailored to the room)**

- Investor/analyst: screening, IC pre-reads, portfolio monitoring, and thesis memos.
- Founder/angel: deal-flow triage, pitch-deck feedback, and quick market sizing.
- Consultant/strategist: framework application, proposal drafting, and synthesis of interviews.
- Sales/BD: account research, tailored outreach, and CRM enrichment (HubSpot).
- Product/ops: PRDs, user-research synthesis, and ticket triage (Jira/Linear).
- HR/recruiting: job descriptions, résumé screening, and structured interview kits.
- Board/governance: pre-read summaries, minutes, and risk-flag scanning.
- Legal/contracts: clause extraction and redline review, with a "verify, don't trust" caveat.

**Governance, trust & verification (your signature theme — and a compliance-minded crowd will value it)**

- When to let autonomy run vs. when to keep a human verify step (your Verify Button framing).
- Grounding answers in sources and demanding citations to suppress hallucination.
- A data-boundary rule for what should never be pasted into a public model.
- Cross-model checking — having a second model verify the first on high-stakes outputs.
- Evaluating and testing your own prompts before relying on them (straight from your TDS work).
- Cost and free-tier-limit awareness so a workflow doesn't quietly break at scale.
- Treating skills and connectors as versioned, reusable assets rather than one-off chats.

Two things I deliberately did _not_ over-research and you may want to verify live before the session, since they shift monthly: the exact free-vs-paid line on NotebookLM's video tiers, and whether any specific finance connector (LSEG, Daloopa) is in the directory your attendees' plans expose — both are easy to confirm in-app the week of.
