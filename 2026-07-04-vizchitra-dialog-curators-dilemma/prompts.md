- People are divided across groups, even given the same audience, purpose, and charts.

- Abundance: AI has made chart production extremely cheap.

- Norms: Explain your assumptions. Disagreement is good. Changing minds is good.
- Prediction: Changing audience and purpose will change judgments more than AI involvement.
- Which is the best chart?: Write your name against it on a post-it (2 min).
- Why this one?: What did you assume the chart was trying to achieve?
- FRAME -> TEST -> CHOOSE -> OWN: Charts are good for someone, something, under particular consequences.
- SHIP/FIX/KILL: **SHIP** for _this_ audience and purpose Y/N? Why? **FIX:** What is the single highest-value change? **KILL:** What would make you refuse publication? **VERIFY:** What is the cheapest decisive approval check? 9 min - write. 1 sheet. You may mark on chart
- Switch tables: Go to the table to your right
- Critique: Find the **strongest** reason SHIP/FIX/KILL decision may be wrong. "The strongest part of your decision is…" "We would change it because…" 7 min
- Discuss: 1. What was your original decision? 2. What did the other group notice? 3. What changed your mind? 4. What evidence would settle disagreement?
- Move your name: If it's judgement changed. You don't have to. But changing your mind from evidence is success. Defending your first impression is not the objective.
- Ownership: - **Before**: anticipating the likely conclusion and the dangerous misreading; - **During**: deciding what evidence, qualification or review is required; - **After**: correcting it visibly and improving the process if it fails.
